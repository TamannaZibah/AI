Multiple Choice (FERPA vs HIPAA)

A college student seeking mental health therapy visits the university’s Student Counseling Center. The counseling records are maintained securely by the university staff. Which federal law primarily governs the privacy of these medical treatment records?

Correct Answer: A

Explanation: According to joint federal guidance, student health and medical records kept by a postsecondary institution are classified as "education records" or "treatment records" under FERPA, exempting them from the HIPAA Privacy Rule.

2
Multiple Choice (Individual Rights)

Under the HIPAA Privacy Rule, do individuals have a legal right to inspect, review, and receive copies of their own Protected Health Information (PHI)?

Correct Answer: A

Explanation: The right of access is a cornerstone of the HIPAA Privacy Rule. Patients are entitled to see and obtain copies of their health records within 30 days (with a single 30-day extension allowed under limited conditions).

3
Multiple Choice (De-Identification)

An intern at a clinical laboratory is preparing an academic poster presentation. Which of the following sets of data is successfully de-identified under HIPAA's "Safe Harbor" method and is therefore NOT considered PHI?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: To be de-identified under the Safe Harbor method, 18 specific identifiers (including names, full dates, geographic data smaller than state, ages over 89, and full-face photos) must be entirely removed. Storing a random ID where the link key is completely isolated is compliant.

4
Multiple Choice (PHI Disposal)

Which of the following describes a fully HIPAA-compliant practice for disposing of physical clinical patient lists in a hospital?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: To safeguard patient privacy, physical documents with PHI must be destroyed using methods that render the text unreadable, indecipherable, and unable to be reconstructed (e.g., shredding or pulping). Standard open trash or recycling bins are active breach risks.

5
Multiple Choice (Minimum Necessary)

A student completing a nursing rotation needs to check a patient's medical history to safely prepare for a morning clinical assignment. Applying the "Minimum Necessary" standard, what information is the student permitted to access?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Under the Minimum Necessary Standard, clinical staff and interns must limit their viewing, request, and transmission of PHI to the minimum amount required to fulfill the operational task or educational clinical assignment.

6
Multiple Choice (Academic Case Studies)

A pre-med student shadowing an oncologist wants to use clinical details from a rare patient diagnosis to draft an academic case report. Under what conditions is a signed, written HIPAA Authorization required from the patient?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Under the HIPAA Privacy Rule, if an academic case study is fully de-identified (satisfying Safe Harbor rules) and does not present identifiable health details, patient authorization is not required. If identifiers are kept, patient permission is legally required.

Match each HIPAA Security Rule safeguard category to its correct real-world administrative, physical, or technical action:

A. Developing workplace privacy training, access protocols, and performing regular risk assessments.
B. Locking server room facility doors and placing privacy glare screens over nursing station monitors.
C. Setting up secure end-to-end data encryption, unique user ID logins, and automatic session logouts.

Correct Matching Pairs:

  • A maps to Administrative Safeguard
  • B maps to Physical Safeguard
  • C maps to Technical Safeguard

Matching (HIPAA Regulatory Terms)

Match each key regulatory term to its legally correct definition:

A. A healthcare provider, health plan, or health clearinghouse transmitting health information electronically.
B. A subcontractor or independent contractor who performs services involving PHI on behalf of a clinic or provider.
C. The specific federal regulation stating the strict legal conditions under which PHI may be used or disclosed.
D. An impermissible acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI that compromises security or privacy.

Correct Matching Pairs:

  • A maps to Covered Entity
  • B maps to Business Associate
  • C maps to HIPAA Privacy Rule
  • D maps to Breach
9
Fill In The Blank

The acronym PHI in standard HIPAA compliance regulations stands for Protected _______ Information.

Correct Answer: Health

Explanation: Under HIPAA, PHI is defined as Protected Health Information, which encompasses any individually identifiable health data created, stored, or transmitted by covered entities.

Clinical Scenario Case Study

You are completing a clinical placement at a busy hospital. During a lunch break in the public cafeteria with other students, you want to discuss a patient who was admitted with an unusual diagnosis. Explain how the HIPAA 'Minimum Necessary' standard and rules on patient confidentiality apply here. What precautions must you take to prevent a breach?

Model Grading Rubric:

For full points, a student response must highlight:

  • Public Setting Danger: Discussing clinical details in public cafeteria spaces, elevators, or corridors is an high-risk practice due to overhearing, representing an implicit breach.
  • De-Identification: If case discussion is academically required, all 18 patient identifiers must be completely omitted from discussion.
  • Minimum Necessary: Only share clinically relevant components essential to peer learning, omitting extraneous personal patient data.
  • Safer Alternatives: Restricting detailed learning case dialogue to secure private classroom spaces or formal debrief clinics.
